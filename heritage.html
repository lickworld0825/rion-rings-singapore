Heritage & Meaning

What is Memorial Jewellery?

A centuries-old tradition of carrying a beloved person — or a cherished pet — with you — woven into gold, sealed in platinum, worn close to the heart. From Victorian England to modern Japan, families across cultures have chosen jewellery as the way to keep love alive through time.

"

Memorial jewellery is a piece of fine jewellery that holds a tangible piece of someone you love — a partner, parent, child, friend, or beloved pet — most often a strand of hair or fur — sealed inside or set as part of the design. It is a wearable presence, not a memorial in a frame.

A definition

Why hair?

Of all the ways to remember someone, hair offers something unique — a piece of who they truly were, preserved with no loss of meaning over time.

i

DNA Forever

Hair contains the genetic essence of the person. It is not a memory — it is them.

ii

No Decay

Properly sealed, hair lasts centuries. Egyptian mummies still hold their hair after 4,000 years.

iii

Hygienic & Safe

Hair is keratin — a stable, non-organic-feeling protein. Carries no body fluid, no contamination, no health concern.

iv

Universally Accepted

Hair-sealed memorial jewellery is accepted across most religions, including Islamic, Christian, Buddhist, Hindu, and Shinto traditions.

Who chooses memorial jewellery?

From grief to celebration, from family to beloved pets, our clients come for many reasons. Each piece holds its own story.

i

After Loss

Parents who lost a child. Children who lost a parent. Partners who lost the love of their life. Families who lost a beloved pet. A way to grieve while staying close.

ii

Beloved Pets

Dogs, cats, horses — fur from a beloved companion sealed in gold. For many, pets are family.

iii

Family Heirlooms

Multi-generational pieces holding hair from grandparents, parents, and children — heirlooms designed to pass down.

iv

Personal Milestones

Marking a survived illness, a personal transformation, or a meaningful chapter — with a piece that holds your own hair.

Religious & cultural considerations.

Memorial jewellery has been practised across nearly all cultures and faiths. Below is a general overview — we encourage all clients to consult with their religious or cultural advisors for personal guidance.

Islamic Tradition

For our Muslim clients

Hair is not considered part of the body that must accompany burial (janazah). Once shed or cut, hair can be respectfully kept, gifted, or set into jewellery without violating Islamic principles.

Memorial jewellery containing a loved one's hair is a relatively modern choice in Singapore and across Southeast Asia, embraced quietly by families who wish to keep someone close. As with any matter touching grief and remembrance, we encourage you to consult your imam or family for personal guidance.

Christian Tradition

For our Christian clients

Hair-sealed memorial jewellery has been part of Christian tradition for over 500 years — from medieval reliquaries to Victorian mourning jewellery, beloved by queens and commoners alike.

Across Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant practices, memorial jewellery is widely accepted as a respectful way to remember.

Hindu, Sikh, Other Traditions

For other traditions

Across Hindu, Sikh, Jewish, Bahá'í, and many other traditions, the principle of keeping a tangible reminder of a loved one is widely respected — though specific customs vary.

If you have specific questions about your tradition, message us — we approach every order with respect and openness.

A Note on Religious Guidance Rion is a jewellery atelier, not a religious authority. The information above is offered as general context only. For binding religious guidance, please consult your imam, priest, rabbi, or family spiritual advisor. We respect all faiths and approach every order with care.
